Conference on Financial Economics and Accounting

Conference on Financial Economics and Accounting

Fall 2021 | Indiana University, Bloomington, IN
Kelley School of Business

The Departments of Finance and Accounting at the Kelley School of Business are proud to host the 31st annual Conference on Financial Economics and Accounting, November 5 and 6, 2021, on the beautiful Indiana University Bloomington campus. 

We are excited to announce our conference keynote speakers. Itay Goldstein, who is the Joel S. Ehrenkranz Family Professor in the Finance Department at the Wharton School of Business of the University of Pennsylvania, will present on Friday, November 5. Robert J. Jackson Jr., the Pierrepont Family Professor of Law at the New York University School of Law and former SEC Commissioner, will present on Saturday, November 6.

This annual conference is organized by members of a consortium representing eight universities: Georgia State University, Indiana University, New York University, Rutgers University, Temple University, Tulane University, the University of Maryland, and the University of Toronto. The conference convenes financial economists and accounting scholars, practitioners, and students from around the US to explore emerging concepts and themes in finance and accounting, with a special focus on the intersection of the two fields of research.

The one-and-a-half-day conference is dedicated to the development of all research methods in finance and accounting, including theory, case studies, archival, and experimental work. We invite authors to submit analytical and empirical papers in all areas of finance and accounting. 

For details on paper submission and conference registration, please see the Submission and Registration tabs below.

  • Submissions will be uploaded electronically through Conftool after paying the submission fee.  Once the submission fee has been paid you will receive a confirmation email with the submission URL link. 
  • The submission period is May 10-July 17, 2021.  Make sure to begin your submission with plenty of time to complete it before the deadline of July 17, 2021, 11:59 p.m. EST.
  • Authors of accepted papers are required to attend their session regardless of which day the session is scheduled. 

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Accounting: Financial reporting, disclosure, regulation, valuation, corporate governance, compensation, financial intermediaries, insider trading.
  • Finance: Corporate finance and restructuring, asset pricing, derivatives, market microstructure, financial intermediation, international finance, financial crisis, mutual funds, and hedge funds.

  • The submission fee is $50 per paper.
  • To pay the fee, create an account with the IU Conference and Event Registration Services website.
  • After payment you will receive an email confirmation with the ConfTool URL, create a ConfTool profile to upload your paper submission.
  • There are no refunds.

-Decisions will be made by September 10, 2021.  All submitters will be notified to the email address provided in the submission process.

Register by October 22.

  • General Registration is $150 with reduced cost for PhD students.
  • To pay the registration fee, go to the IU Conference and Event Registration Services website.
  • There will be no refunds.
  • This conference will be fully in-person.

Indianapolis International Airport is about an hour’s drive from Indiana University. You can rent a car or take a shuttle to Bloomington.

GO Express Travel, 800-589-6004, travels between Indianapolis International Airport and Bloomington several times each day. They also have private cars and a shuttle to and from Chicagoland.

Embarque, 800-888-4639, and Classic Touch Limousine, 800-319-0082, offer private, chauffeured cars and limos.
